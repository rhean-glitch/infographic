Way 1: Skin Texture & Lighting

Skin Texture & Lighting

- Skin looks too smooth, plastic-like, or lighting doesn't match the scene.

Tip: Compare with real photos/videos of the person natural skin has pores and shadows.

Way 2: Suspicious Logo

Supicious Logo

- Random letters or non-existing logos that don't match real organizations

Tip: Always check if the logo belongs to a verified or official source.

Way 3: Suspicious Source Channels

Supicious Source Channels

- Content uploaded by new, unknown, or unverified pages/accounts.

Tip: Verify first look for the same news on trusted outlets before believing or sharing

Way 4: Visual Glitch (Hand Anomaly)

Hand Glitch
(Hand Anomaly)

- Hands or fingers appear blurry, misshapen or extra.

Tip: Rewind or pause the video to check for these samll but telling glitches

Way 5: Robotic or Metallic Audio

Robotic or Metallic Audio

- Voice sounds flat, metallic, or mismatched with the person's lips.

Tip: Use earphones to listen closely and confirm if the audio feels real.

Way 6: Unnatural Facial Movements

Unnatural Facial Movements

- Weird blinking, stiff smiles, or lips that don't synch with words.

Tip: If the face looks odd, pause and questions its authenticity.

Stay Sharp in the Age of AI

As AI-generated content becomes more realistic, it's more important than ever to stay alert and think critically. By watching out for these subtle signs like unnatural skin, distorted audio, or suspicious sources you can avoid being misled by fake or synthetic media.
